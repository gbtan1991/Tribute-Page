Ritchie was the head of Lucent Technologies System Software Research Department when he retired in 2007. He was the "R" in K&R C, and commonly known by his username dmr.

  • 1967 - Ritchie joined Bell Labs
  • 1968 - Ritchie was educated at Harvard University, from which he received a Ph.D.
  • 1969 - He helped Ken Thompson design UNIX
  • 1972 - He created the C programming language, inspired by the language BCPL and by Thompson's language
  • 1973 - He therefore modified C, the two rewrote UNIX in C
  • 1980 - Ritchie contributed to the standardization of C
  • 1983 - Thompson and Ritchie received the Turing Award "for their development of generic operating systems theory and specifically for the implementation of the UNIX operating system"
  • 1990 - he was a prime mover in the development of the Plan 9 and Inferno operating systems
  • 2004 to 2007 - Ritchie was employed by Lucent Technologies as head of Lucent Technologies' System Software Research
